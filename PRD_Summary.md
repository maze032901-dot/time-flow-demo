# 1. Product Vision

- **Positioning:** A self-discipline tool that combines a cat's paw aesthetic with a visual timeline.
- **Visual Metaphor:** A cat's paw reaches out and knocks over a water bottle. When a focus session begins, the cat's paw animation on the left moves toward the "task bottle" on the timeline, knocking it over upon contact.
- **Core Logic:** Tasks are categorized into two types: "Focus" and "Todo".

# 2. Page Layout (Desktop Split View)

- **Left Side (80%):** The main workspace. This area includes a date switcher at the top, a vertical timeline (00:00–24:00) in the middle, and a sticky "Smart Status Control Bar" and "Water Bottle Collection Icon" at the bottom.
- **Right Side (20%):** The Task Pool (Backlog). The top section features an "Add Task" button (occupying 20% of the height). The lower section is the task collection area, which supports filtering by type (Focus/General) and scenario (e.g., Work/Study).

# 3. Core Interaction Flow

- **Scheduling:** Drag cards from the right-hand task pool onto the left-hand timeline.
- **Focusing:** Click "Start" at the bottom to trigger the animation of the cat's paw knocking over the water bottle. Once triggered, the task card on the timeline transitions to a "wet" visual state.
- **Settlement:** When a task is completed, the bottle "flies" into the Collection Gallery. If the task is not completed, it automatically bounces back to the right-hand pool and is marked with an "Unfinished" tag.
